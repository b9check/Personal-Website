Project Overview

DRAGONDOCK (Dragon Rendezvous And Gateway for Orbital Navigation and DOCKing) simulates the complete GNC pipeline for autonomous spacecraft proximity operations‚Äîspecifically modeling a SpaceX Dragon capsule approaching and docking with the International Space Station.

The project was developed for Stanford's AA279D: Spacecraft Formation-Flying and Rendezvous, taught by Prof. Simone D'Amico at the Space Rendezvous Laboratory (SLAB). It progresses from foundational orbital mechanics through to a fully integrated navigation-control closed loop.

The simulation operates in Low Earth Orbit at the ISS altitude (~400 km), accounting for J2 gravitational perturbations that cause secular drift in orbital elements. The deputy spacecraft (Dragon) begins at approximately 10 km separation and executes a rendezvous maneuver to achieve docking proximity with sub-meter accuracy.
